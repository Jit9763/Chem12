Introduction

1. Electrolytic Cells

Cells that convert Electrical Energy into Chemical Energy are known as Electrolytic Cells.

💡 Deep Insight:

In nature, some reactions are Non-Spontaneous (अस्वतः प्रवर्तित), meaning they do not occur on their own. To make these reactions happen, we must provide external energy (Electricity).

In an electrolytic cell, this external electrical energy forces the movement of electrons, driving the chemical reaction.

The Process

2. Electrolysis

The process of decomposition of an electrolyte by passing electricity through its solution or molten state, resulting in a Redox reaction, is called Electrolysis.
📉 At Anode The positive electrode where Oxidation occurs.
📈 At Cathode The negative electrode where Reduction occurs.

Electrolysis of Molten NaCl

(Detailed Case Study)

➔ To perform the electrolysis of NaCl, a rectangular vessel is filled with NaCl solution.

➔ Graphite electrodes (Cathode and Anode) are placed in this solution.

➔ Sodium metal is reduced at the Cathode and Chlorine gas is liberated at the Anode.
