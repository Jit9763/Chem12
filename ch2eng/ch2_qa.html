🟢 Short Answer Questions - 10
Q1
What is the function of Platinum in Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE)?
Answer: Platinum electrode is an inert electrode that provides a surface for the exchange of electrons and aids in the adsorption of gas. It does not participate in the reaction itself.
Q2
State Kohlrausch's Law.
Answer: At infinite dilution, the molar conductivity of an electrolyte is equal to the sum of the individual molar conductivities of its cations and anions.
Q3
State Faraday's First Law of electrolysis.
Answer: The mass (W) of a substance deposited at an electrode is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity (Q) passed through the electrolyte. (W = ZQ = ZIt).
Q4
State two functions of a Salt Bridge.
Answer: (1) It completes the electrical circuit. (2) It maintains the electrical neutrality of the solutions in both half-cells.
Q5
What is Electrochemical Series (ECS)?
Answer: A series obtained by arranging various elements in increasing order of their standard reduction potentials is called the electrochemical series.
Q6
Give relationship between specific conductivity (κ) and cell constant (G*).
Answer: Specific Conductivity (κ) = Conductance (G) × Cell Constant (G*). i.e., κ = (1/R) × (l/A).
Q7
Which fuel cell was used in the Apollo Space Program?
Answer: The H₂-O₂ (hydrogen-oxygen) fuel cell was used in the Apollo space program to obtain electrical energy and drinking water.
Q8
Define Corrosion.
Answer: The gradual destruction of metals due to the formation of unwanted compounds (like oxides or sulfides) on their surface in the presence of air and moisture is called corrosion.
Q9
What is the formula for degree of dissociation (α) for weak electrolytes?
Answer: α = λc / λ∞ (where λc is molar conductivity at a given concentration and λ∞ is limiting molar conductivity).
Q10
Write one main difference between Primary and Secondary cells.
Answer: Primary cells cannot be recharged, whereas secondary cells (like lead storage batteries) can be recharged by passing an electric current through them.
🔵 Long Answer Questions - 5
Q1
Explain Nernst Equation and its relationship with Equilibrium Constant (Kc).
Answer:

Nernst Equation shows the dependence of electrode potential on the concentration of electrolytes and temperature.
Equation: E = E° - RTnF ln Q
At 298K: E = E° - 0.0591n log [Oxidized][Reduced]

Relationship with Kc:
At equilibrium, cell potential (E cell) becomes zero (0). Hence:
0 = E° - 0.0591n log Kc
⟹ log Kc = n × E°0.0591
Q2
Describe the working of an Electrochemical cell (Daniel Cell) with a labeled diagram.
Answer:
A Daniel cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy.
Construction: Zn rod immersed in ZnSO₄ solution and Cu rod in CuSO₄ solution.
1. Anode: Zn undergoes oxidation (Zn ➔ Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻). It is the negative terminal.
2. Cathode: Cu²⁺ undergoes reduction (Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ ➔ Cu). It is the positive terminal.
3. Salt Bridge: Maintains ionic balance.
Electrons flow from Anode to Cathode, and current flows from Cathode to Anode.
Q3
Explain the electrochemical mechanism of rusting of iron.
Answer:
Rusting is an electrochemical phenomenon.
1. Anodic area: Pure iron gets oxidized (Fe ➔ Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻).
2. Cathodic area: Oxygen is reduced in the presence of H⁺ (O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ ➔ 2H₂O).
3. Hydration: Fe²⁺ is further oxidized to Fe³⁺ which deposits as hydrated ferric oxide (Fe₂O₃ ⋅ xH₂O), known as rust.
Q4
State and explain both of Faraday's laws of electrolysis.
Answer:
First Law: Mass deposited (W) ∝ quantity of electricity (Q) ⟹ W = ZIt.
Second Law: When same quantity of electricity is passed through different electrolytes in series, the mass of substances deposited is proportional to their equivalent weights (W₁/W₂ = E₁/E₂).
Q5
Write chemical reactions occurring during discharging and charging of a Lead Storage Battery.
Answer:
Discharging:
Anode: Pb + SO₄²⁻ ➔ PbSO₄ + 2e⁻
Cathode: PbO₂ + SO₄²⁻ + 4H⁺ + 2e⁻ ➔ PbSO₄ + 2H₂O
Charging: Applying external voltage reverses these reactions, converting PbSO₄ back to Pb and PbO₂.
🟣 Numericals - 5
Q1
A conductivity cell has a resistance of 100 Ohm. If cell constant is 1.25 cm⁻¹, find specific conductivity (κ).
Solution:
Resistance (R) = 100 Ohm
Cell Constant (G*) = 1.25 cm⁻¹
κ = Cell ConstantResistance = 1.25100
κ = 0.0125 S cm⁻¹
Q2
Calculate the standard Gibbs free energy (∆G°) for a Daniel Cell. (E° = 1.1 V, F = 96500 C)
Solution:
n = 2 (for Zn, Cu), E° = 1.1 V
∆G° = -nFE°
∆G° = -2 × 96500 × 1.1
∆G° = -212,300 J/mol = -212.3 kJ/mol
Q3
Find the EMF of the following cell: Mg/Mg²⁺(0.1M) || Cu²⁺(0.01M)/Cu (E° cell = 2.71 V)
Solution:
Using Nernst Equation:
E = E° - 0.05912 log 0.10.01
E = 2.71 - 0.0295 × log 10
E = 2.71 - 0.0295 = 2.68 V
Q4
How many Faradays of charge are required to reduce 1 mole of Al³⁺ to Al?
Solution:
Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ ➔ Al
n = 3 moles of electrons
Charge (Q) = nF = 3 F (3 × 96500 C)
Q5
Limiting molar conductivities of Mg²⁺ and SO₄²⁻ are 106 and 160 S cm² mol⁻¹ respectively. Calculate λ∞ for MgSO₄.
Solution:
λ∞(MgSO₄) = λ∞(Mg²⁺) + λ∞(SO₄²⁻)
λ∞ = 106 + 160 = 266 S cm² mol⁻¹
