➡️ Electrochemical Series (विद्युत रासायनिक श्रेणी)

💡 Key Characteristics (ECS Points):
  • In the ECS, moving from top to bottom increases the oxidizing power while decreasing the reducing power.

    Li⁺ = Strongest Reducing Agent (प्रबल अपचायक)
    F₂ = Strongest Oxidizing Agent (प्रबल ऑक्सीकारक)

  • Metal at the top of the ECS can displace a metal located below it from its salt solution.

    Zn + CuSO₄ ➔ ZnSO₄ + Cu

  • All metals located above H₂ in the ECS react with acids to liberate H₂ gas.
  • In a galvanic cell, the metal that appears higher in the series acts as the Anode (-) and the one lower acts as the Cathode (+).
  • The metal lower in the ECS precipitates first during electrolysis.

    Ag⁺ > Cu²⁺ > H⁺ > Zn²⁺

  • The oxides of metals located lower in the ECS have higher Thermal Stability.

    Ag₂O > CuO > ZnO

Q: How would you determine the Standard Electrode Potential (SRP) of Mg²⁺ | Mg system?
  • To determine the SRP of Mg²⁺ | Mg, connect it with the Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE) and measure the cell's EMF.
  • A voltmeter or potentiometer is used to measure the EMF.
  • Since the potential of SHE is zero, the cell's EMF will be equal to the SRP (reduction potential) of the other half-cell (Mg²⁺|Mg).
  • Here, we take Mg²⁺|Mg as the Anode (Since its SRP is negative).
Q: Why does the blue color of CuSO₄ disappear when stored in a Zinc container?

Ans: CuSO₄ + Zn ➔ ZnSO₄ + Cu
Zinc is more reactive than copper and displaces it from the solution, causing the blue color to fade.

Q: Can Nickel Sulphate (NiSO₄) solution be stored in an Iron (Fe) container? Explain.

Ans: No. Because Iron (Fe) is more reactive than Nickel (Ni). It will displace nickel from its salt solution, damaging the container.
Fe + NiSO₄ ➔ FeSO₄ + Ni

🔍 Elaborated Explanation
Q: By inspecting the SRP table, name three substances that can oxidize Ferrous ions (Fe²⁺) under suitable conditions.
Detailed Answer:
Oxidizing Fe²⁺ means converting it to Fe³⁺:
Fe²⁺ ➔ Fe³⁺ + e⁻ (E°red = 0.77 V)
Rule: Any substance whose standard reduction potential (E°) is greater than 0.77 V can oxidize Fe²⁺.

According to the table, three major substances are:
  • 1. Chlorine (Cl₂): E° = 1.36 V. Since it is greater than 0.77 V, it oxidized Fe²⁺ easily.
    Cl₂ + 2Fe²⁺ ➔ 2Cl⁻ + 2Fe³⁺
  • 2. Dichromate ion (Cr₂O₇²⁻): In acidic medium, E° = 1.33 V, which is $> 0.77 \text{ V}$.
    Cr₂O₇²⁻ + 14H⁺ + 6Fe²⁺ ➔ 2Cr³⁺ + 7H₂O + 6Fe³⁺
  • 3. Permanganate ion (MnO₄⁻): In acidic medium, E° = 1.51 V. It is a very strong oxidizing agent.
    MnO₄⁻ + 8H⁺ + 5Fe²⁺ ➔ Mn²⁺ + 4H₂O + 5Fe³⁺
Conclusion: If E°substance > E°Fe³⁺/Fe²⁺ (0.77V), it will oxidize Fe²⁺.
➡️ Nernst Equation — Detailed Derivation

Nernst provided an equation to measure the electrode potential at any concentration and temperature relative to the NHE, called the Nernst Equation.

Consider a reversible reaction in a cell:

aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD
(Stoichiometric coefficient) ↖
(Molecule / Atom / Ion) ↑

According to Thermodynamics:

ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln θ     --- ①
Symbols & Constant Values
  • 🔹 ΔG = Gibbs Energy
  • 🔹 ΔG° = Standard Gibbs Energy
  • 🔹 F = Faraday's Constant
  • 🔹 1F = 96500 C/mol
  • 🔹 E = Cell Potential
  • 🔹 E° = Standard Cell Potential
  • 🔹 R = Gas Constant
  • 🔹 R = 8.314 J/K mol
  • 🔹 T = 298 K (Temperature)
  • 🔹 θ = [C]c [D]d[A]a [B]b

We know that:

ΔG = -nFE     --- ②
ΔG° = -nFE°     --- ③

Substituting eq. ② and ③ in eq. ①:

-nFE = -nFE° + RT ln θ

Dividing the entire equation by -nF:

-nFE-nF = -nFE°-nF + RT ln θ-nF

After simplification:

E = E° - RTnF ln θ

Substituting the values of Constants:

E = E° - 8.314 × 298n × 96500 ln θ

Converting Natural Log (ln) to Log₁₀ (ln x = 2.303 log x):

E = E° - 8.314 × 298 × 2.30396500 × log θn
E = E° - 0.0591n log θ
— Nernst Equation
➡️ Nernst Equation for Daniell Cell —

Representation of Daniell Cell:

Zn(s) / Zn+2(aq) || Cu2+(aq) / Cu(s)

Net Cell Reaction:

Zn(s) + Cu2+(aq) ⇌ Zn2+(aq) + Cu(s)

According to Nernst Equation:

E = E° - RTnF ln [P][R]
E = E° - 0.0591n log10 [P][R]

For Daniell Cell (n = 2):

E = E° - 0.05912 log10 [Zn2+] [Cu][Zn] [Cu2+]
📝 Special Note
  • 🔹 For Daniell Cell n = 2
  • 💡 The **activity of pure liquid or solid** substances is always **1**.
  • [Zn] = [Cu] = 1

Substituting the value (1) for solids:

E = E° - 0.05912 log10 [Zn2+][Cu2+]
This equation is the Nernst Equation for the Daniell Cell.

➡️ Calculation of Equilibrium Constant (K) —

Complete Step-by-Step Derivation at Equilibrium:

According to Thermodynamics:
ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln θ
At Equilibrium:
  • 1. ΔG = 0 (No change in free energy)
  • 2. Ecell = 0 (Cell potential is zero)
  • 3. θ = K (Equilibrium constant)
Substituting these values:
0 = ΔG° + RT ln K
RT ln K = -ΔG°
Dividing by RT:
ln K = -ΔG°RT
Converting to log₁₀ (Multiply by 2.303):
2.303 log K = -ΔG°RT
log K = -ΔG°2.303 RT
Equilibrium Constant (K) in Antilog form:
K = antilog ( -ΔG°2.303 RT )
Substituting ΔG° = -nFE°:
log K = nE°0.0591
Note - Determination of Cell Spontaneity:

Since ΔG = -nFE, based on the signs:

➔ Ecell = +Ve (Spontaneous)
➔ Ecell = -Ve (Non-Spontaneous)

Q1: Represent the cell for the following reaction and calculate its Ecell:
Mg(s) + 2Ag+(0.0001M) ➔ Mg2+(0.130M) + 2Ag(s)
(Given: E°cell = 3.17 V)
Ans —
i) Cell Notation:
Mg | Mg2+(0.130M) || Ag+(0.0001M) | Ag
ii) Calculation via Nernst Equation:
E = E° - RTnF ln [Mg2+][Ag+]2
E = E° - 0.0591n log [Mg2+][Ag+]2
Parameters: E° = 3.17V, [Mg2+] = 0.130, [Ag+] = 0.0001, n = 2
E = 3.17 - 0.05912 log 0.130(0.0001)2
E = 3.17 - 0.0295 log (1.3 × 107)
E = 2.96 Volt
Q2: Calculate the equilibrium constant (Kc) for the following reaction:
Cu(s) + 2Ag+(aq) ➔ Cu2+(aq) + 2Ag(s)
(E° = 0.46 V, n = 2)
Ans —
log Kc = nE°0.0591
log Kc = 2 × 0.460.0591
log Kc = 15.5932
Kc = Antilog 15.5932 = 3.919 × 1015
Q3: The standard electrode potential for the Daniell cell is 1.1 V. Calculate standard Gibbs energy (ΔG°) for the reaction:
Zn(s) + Cu2+(aq) ➔ Zn2+(aq) + Cu(s)
Ans —
Parameters: n = 2, F = 96500 C/mol, E°cell = 1.1 Volt
ΔG° = -nFE°cell
ΔG° = -2 × 1.1 × 96500
ΔG° = -212.27 kJ/mol
Q4: Find the EMF of Ni-Ag cell (Ni + 2Ag⁺(0.002M) ➔ Ni²⁺(0.160M) + 2Ag) if E° = 1.05 V.
Answer: Here n = 2.
E = 1.05 - (0.0591/2) log [0.160 / (0.002)²] = 0.91 V (Approx)
