➔ Faraday's Laws of Electrolysis -

1⟩ Faraday's First Law

➔ According to Faraday's First Law, the mass of a substance deposited at an electrode in an electrolyte solution is directly proportional to the charge passed through that solution.

W ∝ Q
W = ZQ
— ①
Q — Charge passed
W — amount of substance deposited
Z — Electro-chemical equivalent
Q = I t
W = Z I t
— ②

If    I = 1 Amp.    t = 1 sec    then    W = Z

"If 1 Amp current is passed for 1 sec in an electrolyte solution, then the mass of substance deposited on electrodes is called its Electro-chemical equivalent."

➔ The ratio of the equivalent weight of a substance and the number of electrons lost or gained in the solution is called the 'Electro-chemical equivalent'.

Z =
E nf
— ③

On putting eq (③) in eq (②)-

W =
E nf
× I t
