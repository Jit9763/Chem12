Salt Bridge (लवण सेतु)
  • A salt bridge is a U-shaped glass tube filled with an agar-agar gel saturated with KCl, KNO₃, NH₄Cl, etc.
  • KCl, KNO₃ etc. are inert electrolytes.
Functions of Salt Bridge (लवण सेतु के कार्य) -
  1. It completes the circuit of the cell.
  2. It maintains the electrical neutrality of both half-cells.
  3. It prevents the generation of liquid-liquid junction potential.
Liquid-Liquid Junction Potential (द्रव-द्रव सन्धि विभव):
When two solutions of electrolytes come in direct contact, a potential is generated at the junction of the two liquids, which is called the liquid-liquid junction potential. Its nature always opposes the potential of the cell.
Standard Electrode Potential (मानक इलेक्ट्रोड विभव)
  • The potential of an electrode in a metal ion solution of 1 mol L⁻¹ concentration at 298K in a half-cell is called the 'standard electrode potential'.
  • It is denoted by E°.
  • If the half-reaction of the cell is expressed as reduction, it is called standard reduction potential, it is denoted by E°red.
  • If the half-reaction of the cell is expressed as oxidation, it is called standard oxidation potential, it is written by E°oxi.
Electromotive Force (विद्युत वाहक बल - EMF)
  • In an open circuit, when no electric current is flowing in the cell, the difference in potentials generated at the electrodes is called electromotive force (emf).
Ecell = Ecathode - EAnode
Difference between Cell Potential and EMF :-
Cell Potential (सैल विभव) EMF (विद्युत वाहक बल)
The potential difference between two electrodes when current flows in the cell. The potential difference when no electric current flows in the cell is called EMF.
The value of potential difference is less than the maximum voltage of the cell. EMF is the maximum voltage of the cell.
Maximum work cannot be obtained from potential difference. Maximum work is obtained from EMF.
Standard Hydrogen Electrode (मानक हाइड्रोजन इलेक्ट्रोड) [SHE]
  • In a standard hydrogen electrode, there is a platinum foil coated with platinum black or platinized platinum in a glass tube.
  • This electrode remains dipped in an acidic solution kept in a beaker and pure hydrogen gas is passed through it at 298K temperature and 1 bar pressure.
  • The concentration of both reduced and oxidized states of hydrogen is kept constant (1 unit).
  • The pressure of hydrogen gas in the solution is 1 bar and the concentration of hydrogen ions is one molar.
  • In SHE, oxidation or reduction occurs on the Pt foil, hence SHE can act as both Anode or Cathode.
H₂ (1 bar) ➔
SHE (Pt/H₂) - 1M H⁺ (HCl)
🚀 How it works: This is a Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE). Pure H₂ gas is bubbled over a platinum foil at 1 bar pressure, submerged in a 1M acidic (H⁺) solution (e.g., HCl).
