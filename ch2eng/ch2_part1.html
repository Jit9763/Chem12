Electrochemistry

Part 1

Electrochemistry
  • Electrochemistry - The branch of chemistry that studies the production of electricity from the energy released during spontaneous chemical reactions, and the use of electrical energy to bring about non-spontaneous chemical transformations, is called electrochemistry.
  • It studies the relationship between electrical and chemical energy and their interconversion.
  • Electrochemistry primarily involves Redox reactions.
Electrochemical Cells
  • An electrochemical cell is a device by which chemical energy is converted into electrical energy.
  • These are also called Voltaic or Galvanic cells.
Galvanic Cell:
  • By this, chemical energy is changed into electrical energy.
  • A spontaneous redox reaction occurs in a galvanic cell.
  • In a galvanic cell, the electrode with higher potential is the Anode and the electrode with lower potential is the Cathode.
  • Oxidation takes place at the Anode and Reduction takes place at the Cathode.
  • In a galvanic cell, the Anode is the negative electrode and the Cathode is the positive electrode.
  • In a galvanic cell, electrons (e⁻) flow from Anode to Cathode in the external circuit, and from Cathode to Anode in the internal circuit.
Construction of a Galvanic Cell
  • To construct this cell, a Zn metal rod is placed in a ZnSO₄ solution and a Cu rod is placed in a CuSO₄ solution, and both are connected by a salt bridge.
  • One half-cell of this cell is the Zn electrode and the other half-cell is the Cu electrode; joining these two half-cells with a KCl salt bridge forms a complete cell.
  • Upon connecting with a metallic wire, the flow of e⁻ occurs from the Zn electrode to the Cu electrode through the external circuit.
  • Zn atoms of the Zn electrode lose e⁻ and enter the solution as Zn⁺² ions, and Cu⁺² ions from the solution start depositing as Cu. By this device, chemical energy is converted into electrical energy.
Mechanism:
At Anode: Zn(s) (oxidation) ➔ Zn+2(aq) + 2e⁻

At Cathode: Cu+2(aq) + 2e⁻ (Reduction) ➔ Cu(s)
Electrode Potential
  • The galvanic cell reaction is a spontaneous redox reaction, meaning the value of Gibbs energy is negative.
  • Upon connecting both electrodes of a galvanic cell, an oxidation reaction occurs at one electrode, called the anode, and a reduction reaction occurs at the other electrode, called the cathode.
Net cell Rexn: Zn + Cu+2 ➔ Zn+2 + Cu
Oxidation Potential -
  • The tendency of an electrode to lose e⁻ into the solution is called oxidation potential.
  • The electrode with a higher tendency to lose e⁻ will have a higher oxidation potential.
Zn ➔ Zn+2 + 2e⁻
Zn/Zn+2 = + 0.76 V
Reduction Potential -
  • The measure of the tendency of an electrode to gain e⁻ from the solution is called reduction potential.
Cu+2 + 2e⁻ ➔ Cu
Cu+2/Cu = + 0.34 V
Electrode Potential -
  • The potential resulting from the separation of charges between a metal electrode and its ion solution is called 'electrode potential'.
