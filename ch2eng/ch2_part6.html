🧪 1. Molar Conductance (λₘ)
  • The conductivity of all the ions produced by dissolving one mole (1 mol) of an electrolyte in a solution is called 'Molar Conductance'.
λm = κ × 1000C
Where κ = Conductivity / Specific Conductance (S cm⁻¹)
C = Concentration or Molarity (mol L⁻¹)
SI Unit of λₘ: S m² mol⁻¹
⚖️ 2. Equivalent Conductance (λₑᵩ)
  • The conductivity of a solution containing 1 gram equivalent (1 gm eq) of an electrolyte.
λeq = κ × 1000N
Note N = Normality of the solution
Unit: S cm² eq⁻¹ or S m² geq⁻¹
📏 3. Cell Constant (G*)
  • The ratio of the distance between electrodes ($l$) and their area of cross-section ($A$) is called the 'Cell Constant'.
G* = lA
Relation We know: $R = \rho \frac{l}{A} = \frac{1}{\kappa} \frac{l}{A}$
Therefore, $R \kappa = \frac{l}{A} = G$

Key Formula: $G = R \times \kappa$
Unit: m⁻¹ or cm⁻¹

⚛️ Kohlrausch's Law

→ According to Kohlrausch's law, the molar conductivity of an electrolyte at infinite dilution is the sum of the molar conductivities of its cations and anions at infinite dilution.

λm∞ = λ+∞ + λ-∞

→ If the number of cations and anions in the electrolyte is more than one:

λm∞ = ν+λ+∞ + ν-λ-∞
ν+ = Number of Cations | ν- = Number of Anions
Examples 1: NaCl
λNaCl∞ = λNa+∞ + λCl-∞
ν+ = 1, ν- = 1
Examples 2: MgCl₂
λMgCl₂∞ = λMg2+∞ + 2λCl-∞
ν+ = 1, ν- = 2

A. Calculating Molar Conductivity of Weak Electrolytes
Step 1: Write dissociation equations for known electrolytes:
① CH₃COOH ⇌ CH₃COO⁻ + H⁺
② CH₃COONa ⇌ CH₃COO⁻ + Na⁺
③ HCl ⇌ H⁺ + Cl⁻
④ NaCl ⇌ Na⁺ + Cl⁻

General equations for Molar Conductivity (λm∞):

λCH₃COOH∞ = λH⁺∞ + λCH₃COO⁻∞ --- (1)
λCH₃COONa∞ = λNa⁺∞ + λCH₃COO⁻∞ --- (2)
λHCl∞ = λH⁺∞ + λCl⁻∞ --- (3)
λNaCl∞ = λNa⁺∞ + λCl⁻∞ --- (4)
Step 2: Combine the equations (Algebraic Manipulation):
Equation Combination:
Eq. (2) + (3) - (4)

Expansion in terms of ions:

(λNa⁺∞ + λCH₃COO⁻∞) + (λH⁺∞ + λCl⁻∞) - (λNa⁺∞ + λCl⁻∞)
Step 3: Final Substitution and Result:

After canceling the spectator ions Na⁺ and Cl⁻:

= λCH₃COO⁻∞ + λH⁺∞
(Which matches Equation ①)

Final Premium Formula:

λCH₃COOH∞ = λCH₃COONa∞ + λHCl∞ - λNaCl∞
