❖ Abnormal Molar Mass (Abnormal Molar Mass) -

  • Solute + Solvent = Ionization/Association = Change in Number of Particles = Change in Colligative Properties
  • Molecular mass of Solute is inversely proportional to Colligative Properties
  • When the experimental Molar Mass is different (less/more) from the true Molar Mass, it is called Abnormal Molar Mass कहलाता है

(A) Dissociation of Solute Particles (वियोजन/आयनन) :     AB ⇌ A⁺ + B⁻

Ionic solutes dissolve in solvent and dissociate into two or more particles. Due to this, the number of particles increases, causing colligative properties to increase, which lowers the molecular mass.

Example 1: If 1 mole of NaCl, CaCl₂, or K₄[Fe(CN)₆] is dissolved in water with 100% ionization, what will be their observed molecular mass compared to actual molecular mass?

Ans (उत्तर) –
NaCl→ Na⁺ + Cl⁻= 2 mole ions
CaCl₂→ Ca²⁺ + 2Cl⁻= 3 mole ions
K₄[Fe(CN)₆]→ 4K⁺ + [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻= 5 mole ions

Since Colligative Property (number of ions) is inversely proportional to Molecular Mass. Colligative Property ∝ 1Molecular Mass
Therefore, ratio of their observed to actual molecular masses will be respectively 1/2 ; 1/3 ; 1/5 .

(B) Association of Solute Particles (संगुणन/संयोजन) :     nA ⇌ (A)ₙ

Two or more particles of a solute associate/join together to form larger molecules. This decreases the total number of particles, causing a decrease in colligative properties and an increase in molecular mass.

Example 2: Why do Formic acid, Acetic acid, and Benzoic acid show double the molecular mass when dissolved in Benzene?

उ० – These carboxylic acids associate in Benzene (a low dielectric solvent) via Hydrogen bonding, forming a Dimer (द्विलक), which decreases the number of particles.

चूंकि अणुसंख्य गुण (कणों की संख्या) अणुभार के व्युत्क्रमानुपाती होते है। अणुसंख्यक गुणधर्म ∝ 1Molecular Mass
Therefore, their observed molecular mass is doubled. E.g.

❖ Van't Hoff Factor (i)

The factor used to determine the extent of dissociation or association is called the Van't Hoff Factor (वांटहॉफ गुणांक).

i =   Normal Molar Mass Abnormal Molar Mass   =   Observed Colligative Property Calculated Colligative Property
i =   Observed Coll. Property Theoretical Coll. Property
i =   Total Moles after Association/Dissociation Total Moles before Association/Dissociation

The value of Van't Hoff Factor (i) predicts the nature of the solute:

  • यदि i = 1 No dissociation/association. The non-volatile solute behaves normally.
  • i > 1 Solute will undergo Dissociation (वियोजन)     (KCl(aq) for i = 1.98)
  • i < 1 Solute will undergo Association (संगुणन)     (बेंजीन में ऐथेनोइक अम्ल for i = 0.5)

❖ Relation b/w Colligative Properties & Van't Hoff Factor :

Relative Lowering of V.P. (RLVP),     P₁° - P₁ P₁°   = i × n₂ n₁
Elevation in B.P.,     ΔTb = i · Kb · m
Depression in F.P.,     ΔTf = i · Kf · m
Osmotic Pressure,     π = i · n₂ · R · T V

Questions -

(1) NH₃, HCl, CO₂, SO₂ solubility in water is higher than H₂, O₂, N₂
Reason – These gases are highly reactive and react with the solvent (water) to form ions. E.g:
NH₃ + H₂O → NH₄OH     ;     HCl(g) + H₂O → HCl(aq)
CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃     ;     SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₄
(2) Oxygen dissolves faster in blood compared to water, why?
Reason – O₂ reacts with Hb present in the blood to form highly active Oxyhemoglobin (HbO₂).
(3) HCl is highly soluble in water but less soluble in benzene, why?
Reason – "Like dissolves like". Both HCl and water are polar, whereas benzene is non-polar.
(4) Solubility of gases in liquids is an exothermic process, how?
Reason – According to Le Chatelier’s principle at constant pressure: Temp ↑ causes Kinetic Energy ↑ so Solubility ↓.

Exception – H₂ and He dissolution absorbs heat (Endothermic). Hence with Temp ↑, their solubility also ↑.
