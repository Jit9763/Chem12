1.1 Concept of Solutions

What is a Solution?

A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances is called a Solution.

In a homogeneous mixture, the composition and properties remain uniform throughout the mixture.

Components of a Solution:

  • Solvent: The component present in a larger quantity, which dissolves the other substance and determines the physical state of the solution.
  • Solute: The component present in a smaller quantity, which is dissolved in the solvent, is called the solute.
Solvent + Solute   ➔   Solution

Properties of a Solution

  • A solution is a single-phase system consisting of more than one component. Based on components, it can be Binary (2), Ternary (3), or Quaternary (4).
  • It is a homogeneous mixture whose composition can only be varied within certain limits.
  • Examples: Sugar in water, lemonade, air, etc.

1.2 Types of Solutions

Based on the physical state of the solvent and solute, solutions are classified into 9 types:

Gaseous Solutions

The solvent is always a Gas.

Gas + Gas (Gas in Gas) Example: Mixture of Oxygen and Nitrogen, air.
Gas (Solvent) + Liquid (Solute) Example: Mixture of Chloroform (CHCl₃) in Nitrogen gas.
Gas (Solvent) + Solid (Solute) Example: Camphor dissolved in Nitrogen gas.

Liquid Solutions

The solvent is always a Liquid.

Liquid (Solvent) + Gas (Solute) Example: Oxygen dissolved in water, Soda water (CO₂ in water), ammoniated water.
Liquid + Liquid (Liquid in Liquid) Example: Ethanol (Alcohol) dissolved in water.
Liquid (Solvent) + Solid (Solute) Example: Sugar or Glucose dissolved in water.

Solid Solutions

The solvent is always a Solid.

Solid (Solvent) + Gas (Solute) Example: Gaseous adsorption on metal surfaces (e.g., Pd + H₂, Ti + N₂).
Solid (Solvent) + Liquid (Solute) Example: Amalgams like Na + Hg, Cu + Hg, Zn + Hg.
Solid + Solid (Solid in Solid) Example: Alloys such as Cu + Au, Brass (Zn + Cu), Bronze (Zn + Sn).
