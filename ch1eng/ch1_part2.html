Expressing Concentration of Solutions
(Chapter 1)

1.3 Introduction to Concentration

The amount of solute dissolved in a specific volume or mass of a solvent is called the Concentration of a solution.

Concentration expresses the Qualitative and Quantitative composition of a solution.

Methods to Express Concentration:

  • Qualitative: Dilute solutions and Concentrated solutions.
  • Quantitative: This provides the exact ratio of solute to solvent. It is divided into three types of units:
    • Weight-Weight Units: Mass percentage, Mole fraction, Molality, ppm (These are independent of temperature).
    • Weight-Volume Units: Mass-Volume percentage, Molarity (These are dependent on temperature).
    • Volume-Volume Units: Volume percentage.

1.4 Mass Percentage (w/W %)

The mass of the solute in grams present in 100 g mass of the solution is called its Mass Percentage.

Mass Percentage (w/W %) =
Mass of solute (g) Total mass of solution (g)
  × 100

Commercial Use (Example):

Commercial bleaching solution contains 3.62 mass percentage of sodium hypochlorite in water.

1.5 Volume Percentage (v/V %)

The volume of the solute in ml present in 100 ml volume of the solution is called its Volume Percentage.

Volume Percentage (v/V %) =
Volume of solute (ml) Total volume of solution (ml)
  × 100

Commercial Use (Example):

A 35% v/V solution of ethylene glycol is used as an antifreeze in car engines. It lowers the freezing point of water to 255.4 K (-17.6 °C).

1.6 Mass-Volume Percentage (w/V %)

The mass of the solute in grams (g) present in 100 ml volume of the solution is called its Mass-Volume Percentage.

Significance / Utility: It is highly useful and commonly used in the medicine and Pharmacy industries.

Mass-Volume (w/V %) =
Mass of solute (g) Total volume of solution (ml)
  × 100

Commercial Use (Example):

For making 500 ml of an aqueous solution of 2% w/V NaCl, we require 10 grams of NaCl. (2 = W × 100 / 500)

Interactive Lab: Pharmacy Calculator (w/V %)

Mass of Medicine (WB) 2 g
Total Solution Vol (VS) 100 ml
2 / 100 × 100
2.0%

1.7 Parts Per Million (ppm)

When a solute is present in trace quantities, it is convenient to express concentration in ppm.

The mass of the solute in grams present in 10⁶ (one million) g mass of the solution is known as ppm (parts per million).

ppm =
Mass of solute (g) Total mass of solution (g)
  × 10⁶
